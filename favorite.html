Authors

Patrica Briggs

Patrica Briggs

Urban Fantasy

Patricia Briggs (born 1965 in Butte, Montana) is an American fantasy author best known for her urban fantasy series about coyote‑shifter and Volkswagen mechanic Mercy Thompson. She began publishing traditional fantasy in 1993 with Masques before shifting to urban fantasy in 2006 with Moon Called, which launched the Mercy Thompson books and became a bestseller. Her work now includes multiple series, graphic novels, and over twenty novels, and she lives in Washington State with her family and a small herd of horses, writing full time.

Ilona Andrews

Ilona Andrews

Urban Fantasy

Ilona Andrews is the pseudonym for husband-and-wife writing team Ilona Gordon (native-born Russian) and Andrew "Gordon" Gordon (former U.S. Army communications sergeant). They met in college English class (Ilona got the better grade), married in 1995, and live in Texas with their two children, dogs, and cats. Debuting with Magic Bites (Kate Daniels #1, 2007), they've written 40+ novels across urban fantasy, paranormal romance, and sci-fi, including NYT #1 bestsellers like Hidden Legacy.

Rick Riordan

Rick Riordan

Mythological Fantasy

Rick Riordan (b. 1964, San Antonio, TX) is #1 NYT bestselling author of Percy Jackson & the Olympians, created as bedtime stories for his ADHD/dyslexic son. Former middle school teacher whose adult mystery Tres Navarre series won Edgar, Anthony & Shamus awards. Lives in Boston with wife Becky & sons Haley & Patrick.

Brandon Mull

Brandon Mull

Young Adult Fantasy

Brandon Mull (b. 1974) is #1 NYT bestselling author of Fablehaven, Beyonders, Five Kingdoms & Candy Shop War. Former comedian/film marketer from California who debuted with Fablehaven (2006). Lives in Utah with wife & four children. Known for magical creatures & epic quests.

Tom Clancy

Tom Clancy

Techno-Thriller

Tom Clancy (1947-2013) pioneered techno-thriller genre with The Hunt for Red October (1984). Former Maryland insurance broker whose Jack Ryan novels sold 100M+ copies. Films include Hunt for Red October & Patriot Games. Passionate military history buff.

The Wizard's Butler

The Wizard's Butler

Urban Fantasy

Unemployed Tavistock Reeves becomes butler to reclusive wizard Grindletop. Discovers hidden magical underworld beneath modern London. Manages mansion, wards off magical threats, uncovers wizard's secrets. Cozy fantasy with fish-out-of-water humor & British charm by Nathan Lowell.

Spy vs. Spy

Spy vs. Spy

Wordless Comic Strip

MAD Magazine's iconic wordless strip by Antonio Prohías (1961-1981). Black Spy vs. White Spy wage endless comical espionage war with booby traps, gadgets, explosions. Beak-headed rivals in hats & suits - timeless slapstick rivalry. Collected in comic books.

Trouble Shooter

Trouble Shooter

Western
